Rasterization Based Simulation of Lens Effects

The topic of my bachelor's thesis was "Rasterization Based Simulation of Lens Effects". The goal was to simulate effects like depth of field, distortion and vignetting in real time. While there are many techniques to approximate each of these effects, it is impractical to combine all of them in a single program. Instead I implemented an older technique introduced in the paper "An Image-Based Model for Realistic Lens Systems in Interactive Computer Graphics" by Heidrich et al. in 1997. This technique is capable of simulating the image formation within a specified lens system and accounts for all the effects mentioned above. Regrettably is too expensive to archive interactive frame rates, even on modern hardware.

Concepts:
  • Lens effects
  • Camera and lens models
  • Newton's method in multiple dimensions
Technologies:
  • C++
  • OpenGL
  • GLSL
  • CMake

Material Models for Sensor Simulation

The topic of my master's thesis was "Material Models for Sensor Simulation". Material models used for rendering have changed a lot over the last few years across all application areas, from real-time graphics in games to offline movie renderings. In the thesis, I review the literature on modern material models, compare different diffuse and specular models and implement a prototype material for Monte Carlo path tracing. The model supports opaque dielectrics, transparent dielectrics and metals.

Concepts:
  • Monte-Carlo Path Tracing
  • Physically Based Rendering
  • Bidirectional Reflectance Distribution Function
Technologies:
  • C++
  • CMake
  • OpenMP

Virtual Reality and RTX Ray Tracing

In this project I worked in a group of four students to develop a small virtual reality game. The gameplay had to be based on real time ray tracing using the Nvidia RTX graphics cards that had just been released. Since both VR and real time ray tracing require lots of computational power, performance was extremely important. In the finished product, the scene is first rendered using rasterization. In a second pass, ray tracing is used to add mirrors and refracting glass. In the game, the player has to look tough a series of mirrors in order to maintain eye contact with an RC car they are controlling.

Concepts:
  • Virtual Reality
  • Real time ray tracing
  • Combining rasterized and ray traced images
  • Collision detection
Technologies:
  • C++
  • Vulkan
  • RTX ray tracing extensions
  • GLSL
  • OpenVR
  • CMake
